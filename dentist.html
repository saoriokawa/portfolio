ONLINE BOOKING PLATFORM

UX Design / Research

Project Overview

I worked on a 3-day design assignment to create an interface that allows users to book an appointment online with the dentist.

My Role

UX Designer


Tools

Balsamiq, Sketch

Design Process

1. Competitive Analysis

First of all, in order to get a general image of the current dentist online booking system, I conducted competitive analysis. I analysed the following two dental clinic websites.

2. User Reseach

In order to identify problems that users are facing, I tried to reach out some people to ask their experience in making an appointment with a dentist.

List of Questions
  1. How do you make an appointment with your dentist?
  2. What do you find uncomfortable, uneasy or troublesome in the process or at the visit to a dentist?
  3. What do you want to communicate with your dentist when you make an appointment?
  4. What feature of any online booking platform do you enjoy using?
  5. What feature of any online booking platform don’t you enjoy using?

User Interview Results

The results of the interviews revealed following characteristics of potential users.

BEHAVIOURS & ATTITUDES
  • Since they are busy, they find it convenient to make an appointment online as they can book at any time.
  • Since they don’t want to wait at the clinic, before they go to the clinic, they check how many patients are there before them via online platform. They decide to arrive to the clinic earlier or later depending on the situation.
  • Some of them make an appointment over the phone or at the clinic.
NEEDS & GOALS
  • “I want to tell my dentist the reason why I visit and the problems I have.”
  • “I want to get an immediate confirmation on booking.”
FRUSTRATIONS
  • They find it troublesome when they have to make a phone call to cancel the appointment.
  • “Timeslot is fixed. So if the treatment doesn’t finish within the timeslot, I would have to book another appointment though it didn’t happen to me.”
  • “Even if I book an appointment, sometimes I still have to wait at the clinic.”

User Stories

Based on the characteristics above, I created some user stories and established a problem statement.

3. User Flow

With the user stories and the problem statement in mind, I started working on making a user flow.

After an object, an entry point of task flow, and success criteria were defined, I made a list of each task that users would need to go through in order to achieve the object.

Based on the task list, the user flow was established.

4. Wireframes

With the user flow as a reference, wireframing process was initiated from low-fidelity wireframes with a pen and paper, followed by mid-fidelity wireframes with Balsamiq and high-fidelity wireframes with Sketch.

In addition to the objective of booking an appointment, I tried to incorporate additional functions/characteristics to meet the users’ needs.

Additional Functions and Characteristics
  • Immediate confirmation of appointment
  • Comment field for patients to communicate their problems
  • Function to check how many people are waiting before you, in case there are delays in the dentist’s schedule
